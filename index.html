Tips For Maintaining Your Newly Refinished Exterior

Read Post

Clean your exterior at least once a month

Exterior refinishing is important to the beauty and longevity of your home. While some people think that they can get away with cleaning their exterior only once a year, this simply isn't enough. Exterior surfaces are constantly exposed to the elements, and over time, this can take a toll. To keep your home looking its best, it's important to clean your exterior at least once a month.

This may seem like a lot of work, but it's really not that bad - especially if you have a pressure washer. A pressure washer will make quick work of any dirt or grime on your siding, windows, or trim. In addition, it's a good idea to wash your gutters and downspouts at least once a month as well.

Inspect your exterior every few months

Exterior home maintenance is often overlooked but it is just as important as interior maintenance. Exterior damage can cause water to seep into your home, which can lead to serious problems like wood rot and mold growth. Inspecting your exterior every few months for any chips or cracks that need to be repaired can help prevent these costly problems.

Apply a coat of sealant every two years

Exterior refinishing is the process of applying a new coat of paint or sealant to the outside of your home. This can help to protect your home from the elements and keep it looking its best. However, it is important to note that exterior refinishing is not a substitute for proper maintenance.

In order to properly protect your home, you should apply a new coat of sealant every two years. This will ensure that your home is properly protected from the sun, wind, and rain. In addition, it is important to repair any damage to your home's exterior before refinishing. By taking these steps, you can help to extend the life of your home and keep it looking its best for many years to come.

Replace damaged roof shingles immediately

Replacing damaged roof shingles is an important part of exterior home maintenance. If you wait too long, water can damage the roof deck, insulation, and even the framing of your home. So if you notice any missing, cracked, or curling shingles, it's best to replace them right away. There are a few different ways to go about replacing roof shingles.

You can do it yourself, which can be a bit tricky if you're not familiar with roofing terminology or techniques. Or you can hire a professional to do it for you. If you decide to do it yourself, be sure to take into account the weather conditions and the length of the job. It's best to do a roof replacement in the summer when the weather is warm and dry. And it always takes longer than you think it will! So be prepared for a few days' worth of work.

Keep trees and bushes trimmed back

When it comes to exterior refinishing, one of the most important things you can do is keep your trees and bushes trimmed back. This will help to ensure that your home's facade is clearly visible, and it will also make the property look neater and more organized.

If you're not sure how to trim your trees and bushes properly, be sure to consult a professional. They will be able to give you specific instructions on how to maintain the foliage around your home so that it enhances your exterior refinishing project rather than detracting from it.

Sweep the driveway and sidewalks regularly

Keeping your driveway and sidewalks clean is an important part of home maintenance Not only does it make your home look nicer, but it also helps keep it in good condition. Debris can damage the surface of your driveway or sidewalk, so it's important to sweep them regularly. If you have a paved driveway, you can use a leaf blower to keep the debris cleared off. If you have a concrete or brick driveway, you'll need to use a broom to sweep it clean.

Summary

Make sure to avoid harsh chemicals or solvents and use a soft cloth to clean the surface. Be careful not to let any water sit on the finish and make sure to reseal it every few years.
